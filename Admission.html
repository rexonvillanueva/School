General Admission Requirements


Graduates of foreign and Philippine high schools accredited by the Department of Education (DepEd) may be admitted as freshmen into the University based on the following: Performance in the NewG University Admission Test (NewG CAT); and Weighted average of final grades obtained in high school. A total combined score of 1,200 for the verbal and mathematics subtests in the Scholastic Aptitude Test (SAT) will also qualify an applicant for automatic admission as freshman to the University.

The NewG CAT


Those who wish to study at the university take the Admission Test (NewG CAT). It is administered on the first weekend of August every year. The NewG CAT is a four-hour long examination consisting of subtests on language proficiency, reading comprehension, mathematics and science. The NewG CAT is in English and Filipino. Standardized scores on these subtests are combined with the weighted average of final grades in the first three (3) years of high school to determine qualification into UP. Every year, thousands of senior high school students take the NewG CAT exam at UP constituent units and designated exam centers across the country. In 2010, over 65,000 examinees took the NewG CAT.
